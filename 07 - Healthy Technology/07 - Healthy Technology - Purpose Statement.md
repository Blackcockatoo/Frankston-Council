# 07 - Healthy Technology
## Purpose Statement

## Charter thesis

Technology should give attention back.

Frankston 2035 - Art That Lives treats technology as a support layer for real places, real people, and real creativity. The screen is not the artwork. The ecosystem is the artwork.

Healthy technology is technology that helps a person notice, make, learn, connect, or participate - then lets them leave the screen with more attention than they arrived with.

## Why this charter exists

This section defines what Blue Snake Studio means by healthy technology and how that standard applies to children, schools, libraries, families, public art, and council-supported pilots.

The project will use lightweight digital tools where they help the public access learning, creativity, and place-based participation. It will not use digital tools to harvest attention, collect child data, mimic gambling, push endless consumption, or make Frankston feel like a content feed.

## What healthy technology means here

Healthy technology is:

- short-session by design
- no-login by default
- privacy-first, especially around children
- useful in classrooms, libraries, homes, and public places
- accessible to people with different abilities, devices, literacy levels, and time limits
- connected to offline making, walking, noticing, drawing, talking, learning, or sharing
- clear about what it does, what it measures, and when the experience ends

Healthy technology is not:

- infinite scroll
- dark patterns
- gambling mechanics
- child profiling
- compulsory accounts
- attention traps
- novelty for its own sake
- a replacement for teachers, artists, families, libraries, or local places

## Who it protects

This charter protects:

- children from attention-extractive design and unnecessary data collection
- families from confusing or manipulative digital experiences
- schools from risky tools that create privacy, duty-of-care, or classroom management problems
- libraries from becoming support desks for unclear technology
- Council from supporting projects that look creative but create hidden safety or governance issues
- Frankston from becoming the backdrop for a tech product instead of the hero of the project

## Parent test

Every child-facing feature should pass this sentence:

"This tool helps my child notice or make something in Frankston, then it clearly ends."

If the feature cannot be explained to a parent in one sentence, simplify it.

## Core position

Blue Snake Studio is the delivery engine, not the centre of the story.

The job of the technology is to help Frankston become more creative, more connected, more observant, and more confident in its own public imagination.

If a feature steals attention, cut it.

If it needs child data, redesign it.

If it encourages compulsive use, reject it.

If it does not lead back to place, people, learning, or making, it does not belong in Frankston 2035.
