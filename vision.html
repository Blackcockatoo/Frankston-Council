Vision

Useful public art for a more creative Frankston.

Frankston 2035 is a practical civic creativity framework. It connects art, place, schools, public memory, healthy technology, and community participation so more people can become makers.

What it is

A launch platform for small, useful pilots.

Frankston 2035 is not a single mural, app, festival, or brand campaign. It is a way to turn public creativity into repeatable local infrastructure.

Each pilot should be cheap enough to try, useful enough to matter, and clear enough for a councillor, teacher, artist, community worker, parent, or funder to understand quickly.

Why it matters

Frankston already has the ingredients.

Place

The foreshore, pier, station, parks, schools, libraries, markets, and public walls are already rich civic material.

Public memory

Local nostalgia matters when it becomes a bridge into new work, not just a sentimental archive.

Schools

Classrooms can turn public art into writing, observation, design, local history, music, mapping, and technology lessons.

Libraries

Libraries can host print packs, safe creative prompts, family sessions, and accessible alternatives to QR-only experiences.

Artists

Artists become civic collaborators: making work that invites learning, response, and local participation.

Healthy tech

QR and web layers should send people back to place: short prompt, offline action, optional reflection, finish.

The core loop

The ecosystem is the artwork.

  1. Start with a real Frankston place: wall, pier, school, library, market, station, or walking route.
  2. Add a small creative prompt that can be used without special equipment.
  3. Use technology only as a doorway: QR or link, short session, clear ending.
  4. Invite people to make something offline and share only through safe, moderated pathways.
  5. Review what worked, what failed, and whether the next pilot has earned its place.

Next obvious step

Choose one first pilot that can run without cultural overclaiming, child data, or a large budget.

The safest first candidate is a Library Creative Pack or school-friendly print pathway. It can prove the method before the project adds public QR layers or larger site permissions.
