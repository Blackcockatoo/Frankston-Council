Pilot page

Five first moves under $500 each.

These are seed versions, not finished programs. Each one is small enough to test, honest enough to evaluate, and practical enough for a councillor, teacher, artist, librarian, or funder to understand quickly.

The point is not to launch everything. The point is to choose one real place and learn.

Pilot 01

Living Mural Lesson Wall

One existing artwork becomes a low-prep learning surface.

Seed version under $500
One A3 weatherproof QR sign, one lightweight web lesson, one printable worksheet, one teacher feedback form, and a small poster/handout run.
Partners
Council public art staff, one local school or library group, the original artist where relevant, Blue Snake Studio as builder.
Permissions
Artwork/copyright permission, site/signage approval, school participation consent, and Traditional Owner guidance before any cultural material is included.
Next step
Nominate one candidate mural or foreshore wall and ask the artist/site owner whether a single-term lesson layer can be tested.

Pilot 02

Frankston Creativity Trail

A short walk that turns public space into a sequence of creative prompts.

Seed version under $500
Three printable trail stops, three simple web prompt pages, a downloadable map, and a small batch of library or visitor-centre trail cards.
Partners
Libraries, visitor information, local artists, youth programs, council placemaking or foreshore teams.
Permissions
Temporary signage approval, image or artwork permissions, accessibility review, and moderation rules for submitted community work.
Next step
Choose three walkable stops near an existing civic or foreshore route and test the prompts as paper cards first.

Pilot 03

Scan Less / Notice More Walk

A healthy-technology walk where scanning is brief and attention returns to place.

Seed version under $500
Ten noticing prompt cards, one landing page, one downloadable worksheet, and a simple facilitator sheet for teachers or community workers.
Partners
Schools, libraries, youth workers, health promotion staff, family services, walking groups.
Permissions
Site use approval if run as an event, guardian/school processes for children, and privacy rules that avoid names or personal data.
Next step
Run one 20-minute trial with a class, library group, or family walk, then check it against the Healthy Technology Charter.

Pilot 04

Sister City Song + Poster Exchange

One replyable cultural artifact between Frankston and Susono-facing participants.

Seed version under $500
One song or chant prompt, one poster template, one class response sheet, one review-ready exchange brief, and a small digital gallery page.
Partners
Sister-city relationship custodians, one interested school or community group, language teachers, council international/community staff.
Risks
Tokenism, translation errors, uneven participation, schedule mismatch, or implying an official program before approval exists.
Next step
Send a one-page invitation brief to the existing sister-city contacts asking what kind of exchange would be welcome.

Pilot 05

Community-Made Resource Library

A small shared shelf of printable prompts, worksheets, and maker resources.

Seed version under $500
Twelve printable resources, one static library page, one contribution form, one 60-minute library table or workshop, and a basic moderation checklist.
Partners
Libraries, teachers, neighbourhood houses, youth programs, local artists, community workers.
Permissions
Contributor licence, child-safe publishing process, privacy review, attribution rules, and cultural protocol checks for place-based content.
Next step
Host one resource table at a library or school event and ask participants which templates are genuinely useful.
