Hero Diagram / Council Pitch Path

FRANKSTON 2035 — ART THAT LIVES

The greatest public artwork is not a mural. It is a community that learns to create.

Focus:
Start here

Frankston

The place is the hero. The system starts with local walls, walks, classrooms, libraries, stories, problems, and people.

Public anchors

Public art / schools / libraries

  • Existing places become entry points.
  • Learning happens in public, not just online.
  • Partners host small tests before big claims.
Light tools

QR / packs / healthy tech

  • No login by default.
  • Short prompts with clear endings.
  • Printable fallback for every screen path.
Participation

Children / families / artists

  • Create, then leave the screen.
  • Adult-led family and classroom modes.
  • Artists and educators shape useful prompts.
Result

New creators

People do not just consume art. They make, notice, share, repair, teach, and return with others.

Civic return

Stronger Frankston

The artwork is the ecosystem: more creative confidence, safer pilots, better local stories, and visible civic pride.

What This Compresses

  • Public art becomes a doorway into learning and making.
  • Technology supports attention, then sends people back into the real place.
  • Blue Snake Studio is the delivery engine and proof source, not the headline.
  • First Nations work remains protocol-only until guided by appropriate Traditional Owner organisations.
