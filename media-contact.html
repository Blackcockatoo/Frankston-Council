What this proposal is

A practical creative framework — not an announcement.

Frankston 2035 proposes a way to give public art a second life as teaching, community participation, and cultural exchange infrastructure. It connects existing Frankston assets — murals, the foreshore, library spaces, schools — to a low-cost, healthy-technology activation layer.

The site you are reading is the seed kit: a working document for review by council, schools, and community stakeholders. Nothing here is live, funded, or scheduled.

What it is: A proposal and concept kit ready for pilot review.

What it is not: An approved council program, a live school curriculum, an endorsed First Nations partnership, or a confirmed sister-city exchange with Susono.

For media

Reporting on Frankston 2035.

If you are reporting on this proposal, please note the following:

  • This is an independent proposal by Blue Snake Studio, not a Frankston City Council initiative.
  • No council funding, endorsement, or approval exists at this stage.
  • No formal school partnership or curriculum approval exists at this stage.
  • The Frankston–Susono sister-city relationship is real; a creative exchange program using it is proposed, not confirmed.
  • First Nations references on this site are protocol-only. No cultural partnership, content rights, or community approval is implied.

Blue Snake Studio is available to answer questions, provide background, and clarify what is proposed versus what is confirmed.
