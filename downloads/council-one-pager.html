The proposal

Public art that keeps working after the paint dries.

Frankston 2035 — Art That Lives is a proposal to give Frankston's existing public art a second life as teaching, community participation, and cultural exchange infrastructure. It connects murals, foreshore walks, library spaces, schools, and the Frankston–Susono sister-city relationship into a low-cost, practical creative system.

It is not a new mural, a new festival, a new app, or a branding campaign. It is a lightweight activation layer for what is already there — testable in a single school term for under $500.

The framework operates through four loops: a mural becomes a lesson; a trail becomes a noticing exercise; a poster becomes a two-way prompt; a viewer becomes a maker. Each loop is designed to be tested cheaply before scaling.

The immediate ask

Three things. No budget decision required today.

The ask to council at this stage is not a funding commitment. It is three small steps:

  • 1
    Note this proposal. Indicate whether there is appetite to proceed to a Stage One pilot. No funding is committed at this stage. A note of interest is enough to advance.
  • 2
    Nominate one candidate location. Identify a single existing mural, foreshore site, or library trail that could host a one-term pilot layer. Council nominates; Blue Snake Studio scopes the specific pilot.
  • 3
    Request the full pilot project sheets. Blue Snake Studio will prepare a detailed, scoped Stage One brief for council review. This is the next step if appetite exists.

Stage One scope

One site. One term. One honest test.

Stage One is a single-term pilot at one Frankston site. It is designed to be low-cost, low-risk, and straightforward to evaluate.

Duration One school term (approximately 10 weeks)
Cost Under $500 for materials, signage, and web prompt page
Scale One mural or site, one school or community group

What Stage One includes: a single weatherproof QR sign or trail card near an existing mural; a lightweight web prompt page (no login, no student data); a printable worksheet for classroom use; and a teacher or community group feedback form at end of term.

What Stage One does not include: new murals, new artworks, new technology platforms, council staff time beyond site permission, or any ongoing cost commitment beyond the one-term trial.

What this is not

A proposal, not an announcement.

  • An approved council program or funded initiative
  • A formal school curriculum or Education Department resource
  • A First Nations partnership, language claim, or cultural endorsement
  • A confirmed creative exchange program with Susono
  • A commitment to scale before one small test has been completed and evaluated

Everything on this brief and the accompanying website is a proposed framework for review. Nothing is live, funded, or scheduled. Council notes this proposal; any subsequent action requires a separate approval process.

Next steps

What happens after council notes the proposal.

  1. Council notes the proposal and nominates a pilot candidate site.
  2. Blue Snake Studio prepares a scoped Stage One brief for that site.
  3. Council reviews and decides whether to proceed to a fully scoped pilot with budget.
  4. If yes, Stage One runs for one term. Independent evaluation at end of term.
  5. If yes to Stage One outcome, prepare Stage Two brief. If no, document learnings and stop.
