Frankston 2035 — Art That Lives

Public art for a more creative Frankston.

Frankston 2035 is a practical civic creativity framework. It proposes giving Frankston's existing public art — murals, foreshore spaces, library displays, schools — a second life as teaching, participation, and cultural exchange infrastructure.

It is not a single mural, app, festival, or brand campaign. It is a way to turn public creativity into repeatable local infrastructure.

Wall to lesson

Public art becomes curriculum.

A mural becomes a worksheet, classroom prompt, library activity, or local maths and pattern exercise — used by real teachers in real classrooms.

Walk to attention

The screen is a doorway, not a destination.

QR codes and trail cards point people back to the place: look up, notice, sketch, listen, then keep walking.

Exchange to artifact

Sister-city material that invites reply.

A replyable song, poster, class pack, or bilingual learning prompt — reviewed and approved, not decorative symbolism.

Viewer to maker

The audience becomes the creator.

Residents and students add work back into the system through guided, moderated, low-risk contribution pathways.

The ask to Council — three things, no budget decision needed today:

  1. Note this proposal and indicate whether there is appetite to proceed to a Stage One pilot.
  2. Nominate a single candidate location — an existing mural, foreshore site, or trail — for the pilot.
  3. Request the full pilot project sheets for a detailed, scoped Stage One scope.

Status: Proposed and awaiting review. Nothing on this flyer is a confirmed council program, funded initiative, or approved partnership.
