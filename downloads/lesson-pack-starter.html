Lesson Pack Starter

Notice. Make. Share safely.

A 30-minute classroom activity that works for any year level with local adjustment. No login. No student accounts. No personal data collected.

Independent proposal by Blue Snake Studio. Not an official school curriculum or Education Department resource.

How it works

Three steps. One local prompt. One made thing.

Each session has three phases. The digital layer (if used) supports only the notice step — the rest happens on paper or in person.

  1. Notice (10 minutes). Use a local prompt — a mural, a foreshore view, a building detail, a station route — to focus student attention. Ask: what do you see? What do you feel? What does this place remind you of?
  2. Make (15 minutes). Students create something in response: a sentence, a drawing, a pattern, a map, a word list, a short poem. No screen needed for this step.
  3. Share safely (5 minutes). Students share within the classroom only. No public posting, no student accounts, no personal data shared. Teacher decides whether the work goes further — see the Student Artwork Pathway and Permission Checklist.

Local prompts

Pick one. Adjust for your year level and class.

Foreshore

The bay at this time of year

What is the bay doing right now? What does it look like from where you stand? Draw it from memory or describe it in three lines.

Mural

A wall that asks a question

Find a public mural near the school. What is it saying? What would you add? What question would you put on a wall?

Place

My route to school

Draw or describe the route from home to school. What do you pass? What do you notice? What do you usually miss?

Community

A person who makes Frankston better

Name someone — a person you know, a business, a volunteer — who makes the local area better. Write three sentences about what they do and why it matters.

Teacher notes

Use this as a starting point, not a script.

This pack is designed to be adapted. A Year 2 class will use shorter prompts and simpler responses than a Year 10 class. The activity works equally well in English, Visual Art, Humanities, or as a homeroom starter.

Before the session:

  • Choose one local prompt from the list above, or write your own.
  • Print this sheet or display it on a projector — no student devices needed.
  • Decide in advance whether any student work will go outside the classroom (if yes, complete the Permission Checklist first).

During the session:

  • Keep the notice step to 10 minutes. A tight time limit helps students focus.
  • Let students choose their response format — sentence, drawing, map, pattern, list.
  • Share in pairs or small groups before the whole class.

After the session:

  • Collect student work or display it in the classroom.
  • If you want to display publicly, follow the Student Artwork Pathway.

Before going public

Check these before any student work leaves the classroom.

  1. Confirm there is no personal data (full names, addresses, photos of students) in the work.
  2. Complete the school's standard permission process for displaying student work.
  3. Gather family consent if work will appear on any public display or website.
  4. Review the Student Artwork Pathway and Permission Checklist.
  5. Do not post student work online without explicit school and family approval.
