Permission Checklist

Before any student work goes public.

A pre-flight review for teachers and coordinators. Complete all items before any student creative work appears outside the classroom. Print and keep on file for each display or activity.

Independent proposal by Blue Snake Studio. Not an official Education Department document. Always check your school's own consent and privacy policies — they take precedence over this checklist.

Part 1 — School policy

Confirm the school's own requirements first.

Tick each item when confirmed. Do not proceed to the next section until Part 1 is complete.

  • ☐ I have checked the school's student media and display policy.
  • ☐ The display format (corridor, library, external venue, website) is listed as acceptable under school policy.
  • ☐ I have confirmed the school's consent process for student work displayed publicly (separate from the class activity consent).
  • ☐ The school's data privacy obligations (Australian Privacy Principles, or state equivalent) allow this type of display.
  • ☐ The relevant deputy principal, coordinator, or principal is aware of the display.

Part 2 — Content review

Review each piece before it moves forward.

  • ☐ No student's full name appears on the public-facing version of the work.
  • ☐ No student photo is included without specific photo consent for this display context.
  • ☐ No home address, school year, or other identifying information is visible.
  • ☐ The work does not include content that could embarrass or harm the student.
  • ☐ The work respects cultural communities, identities, and places represented in the content.
  • ☐ If the work references other people, places, or communities, those references are accurate and respectful.
  • ☐ The work is accessible: it can be understood without needing additional audio or visual support that is not provided.

Part 3 — Family consent

Confirm consent for the specific context.

Consent for one context does not automatically transfer to another. A family who consented to a classroom display has not necessarily consented to a website.

  • ☐ A plain-English consent note has been sent to the family or guardian describing: what the work is, where it will be displayed, how long it will be displayed, whether names will appear, and who will see it.
  • ☐ The family or guardian has responded and confirmed consent (email, signed note, or school consent platform).
  • ☐ The consent covers the specific display location and format (corridor, library, external venue, online).
  • ☐ If the display is online, the family has been specifically told the work will be publicly accessible on the internet.
  • ☐ Families who did not respond or who declined have had their child's work removed from the display.

Part 4 — Digital layer (if used)

Only applies if a QR code or website is involved.

Skip this section if the display is physical only (corridor wall, printed poster, library display with no QR code).

  • ☐ The QR code destination is a fixed URL that has been reviewed by an adult before going live.
  • ☐ The destination page does not require a student login or collect student personal data.
  • ☐ The destination page has a clear ending — it does not encourage students to keep scrolling or return repeatedly.
  • ☐ The destination page has no public comment section, student forum, or submission form by default.
  • ☐ The destination has been checked against the Healthy Technology Charter.
  • ☐ An adult (teacher or coordinator) is responsible for monitoring the digital layer and can remove it quickly if needed.

Sign-off

Teacher and coordinator sign-off before public display.
