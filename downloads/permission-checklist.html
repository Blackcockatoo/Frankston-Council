Permission checklist — Frankston 2035 proposal

Before anything goes public.

Complete this checklist before printing any sign, posting any public resource, or launching any QR layer. This is a proposal planning tool, not a legal document.

Copyright and artwork

  1. Contact the original artist. Explain the lesson or pilot concept. Get written agreement before any sign, worksheet, or web page references the work.
  2. Check who owns or manages the wall site. Get written agreement for temporary signage. Allow 2–4 weeks for approvals.
  3. Do not reproduce the artwork in detail on a public resource without the artist's explicit approval.

Cultural protocol

  1. Does the artwork, site, or content include any First Nations reference? If yes — stop. Do not proceed without guidance from an appropriate Traditional Owner organisation.
  2. Does the site have cultural or historical significance? Seek advice before installing any signage or content.
  3. Do not imply First Nations approval through imagery, language, or framing unless that approval genuinely exists.

School and family consent

  1. Excursion consent: handled through the school's standard process. No extra form needed from the pilot unless student work will be publicly displayed.
  2. If student work will be displayed publicly: obtain separate written consent from the school and family. Use first name or anonymous credit only.
  3. Do not collect student surnames, email addresses, face photos, home locations, or account details.

Signage and QR

  1. Temporary signage approval confirmed from site owner or council.
  2. QR code tested on at least two phones before the sign is printed. Works without the QR code as a worksheet fallback.
  3. Sign includes: project name, disclaimer ("Independent proposal — not an official Frankston City Council publication"), contact email.
  4. No commercial links, sign-up prompts, or tracking pixels on the linked page.

Sister-city and international content

  1. Do not publish any Frankston–Susono exchange content without review from existing sister-city relationship custodians.
  2. Do not imply official participation by the City of Susono unless formally confirmed.

If any box is not ticked

Do not proceed with that item. A well-permissioned small pilot is better than a fast, under-approved one. This protects the artist, the school, the community, and the integrity of the proposal.
