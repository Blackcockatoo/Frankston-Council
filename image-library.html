Image Library

Assets, moodboards, and work in progress.

All campaign images for Frankston 2035 — Art That Lives are categorised below by their readiness for public use. Some are clean assets ready for distribution. Some are concept drafts for internal review. Some contain significant embedded text or design elements that need editorial sign-off before public distribution.

Images in categories B and C should not be distributed as official campaign material without review. None of the assets on this page imply Frankston City Council endorsement.

A — Public-ready

Clean, distributable. Suitable for sharing, printing, and media use as-is.

B — Moodboard

Concept drafts for internal review. Show the direction, not the finished work.

C — Needs review

Text-heavy or layout-intensive. Not ready for public distribution without editorial sign-off.

Why it matters

Images with large amounts of text can be misread as official council material. Categories prevent that risk.

C — Needs review / not yet public-safe

Text-heavy or layout-intensive. Requires editorial sign-off.

Why this category exists: Images with large amounts of embedded text — particularly design layouts, campaign boards, and branding kits — can be misread as official council material when shared out of context. These assets need a clear caption or redesign before public distribution. Do not use them as standalone images in social media, press, or community presentations without review.

Frankston is the Hero — Campaign Board

Text summary: This board presents the proposal's core framing: Frankston as hero, Blue Snake Studio as maker, and the ecosystem-as-artwork philosophy. The embedded text makes it unsuitable for public sharing without editorial context — the framing language could be mistaken for official council communication. Use the Start Here page or Vision page instead for public-facing summaries.
