Pilot Decision Sheet / Stage One

Choose the first safe test. Do not pitch the whole system.

Frankston 2035 becomes credible when one useful pilot can be trialled, measured, and improved without overclaiming partnership, culture, cost, or technology.

Recommended first pick

Library Creative Pack

It is the safest first pilot because it can be print-first, adult-supported, low-cost, no-login, no-child-data, and easy to review before any public tech layer expands.

Decision lens

Start where permissions are light, the fallback is printable, and success can be observed without collecting child data.

Library Creative Pack

Risk: low Cost: tiny 30/60/90: strong

Printable prompts and optional QR doorway for families to create, notice, and share locally from a library setting.

Council askSupport a library scoping conversation and one supervised micro-trial.

PermissionsLibrary site approval, reviewed prompt text, rights-cleared images, plain privacy note.

Why firstUseful without tech. Easy to stop, revise, and prove.

Living Mural QR Layer

Risk: medium Cost: small 30/60/90: workable

A QR prompt layer attached to one approved public-art site so viewers become makers, learners, and return visitors.

Council askIdentify one suitable wall/site and review a draft prompt path.

PermissionsArtist/site permission, accessibility review, maintenance owner, no child data.

Best roleStrong second pilot after the library pack proves the method.

Healthy Tech Mini-App

Risk: medium Cost: small/medium 30/60/90: conditional

A no-login, short-session web tool that gives a prompt, supports an offline action, then clearly ends.

Council askPermit a demo review only after proof checks pass.

PermissionsPrivacy note, accessibility check, hosting plan, child-safety review, printable fallback.

GateMust prove no child data, no dark patterns, and a clear exit prompt.

Frankston x Susono Classroom Pack

Risk: high dependency Cost: small/medium 30/60/90: proposal only

A proposed classroom exchange pack centred on real exchange, not symbolic decoration or assumed relationship access.

Council askNone yet beyond permission to explore the correct relationship pathway.

PermissionsSister-city custodians, schools, parent permissions, music/image rights, review language.

StatusProposed exchange only until confirmed by the right people.

Respect Through Participation Pathway

Risk: protocol gated Cost: tiny/small 30/60/90: protocol only

A process map for respectful engagement. It is not First Nations content, approval, endorsement, language, symbols, or story.

Council askSupport protocol-only scoping with correct Traditional Owner guidance.

PermissionsAppropriate Traditional Owner organisations before any cultural content or claims.

StatusProtocol pathway only - no permission implied.

Success looks like

One small pilot is used, understood, reviewed, and improved. Participants create something offline.

Failure looks like

The project asks for the whole vision, implies approvals, collects child data, or needs a long explanation.
