# 12-Slide Council Deck Scaffold
## Frankston 2035 - Art That Lives

Status: draft scaffold for council-readable pitch compression. This is not a finished slide deck and does not imply council approval, funding, partnership, First Nations approval, or Susono participation.

Purpose: make the project understandable in 90 seconds, credible in 5 minutes, and pilot-worthy in 15 minutes.

---

## Slide 1 - Title

**Headline:** Frankston 2035 - Art That Lives

**On-slide points**
- A small pilot for civic creativity.
- Frankston is the hero.
- Blue Snake Studio is the delivery engine.

**Visual:** Hero Diagram / system map.

**Next obvious step:** agree this is a pilot conversation, not a whole-program approval.

---

## Slide 2 - The Problem

**Headline:** Public art can become decoration when it does not create participation.

**On-slide points**
- People see art, but do not always get a path to make, learn, or return.
- Children and families need low-pressure ways into creativity.
- Tech often steals attention instead of sending people back into place.

**Visual:** Simple before state: wall / screen / viewer separated.

**Next obvious step:** name one local setting where participation could be made easier.

---

## Slide 3 - The Opportunity

**Headline:** Frankston can turn public creativity into useful civic infrastructure.

**On-slide points**
- Connect public art, schools, libraries, artists, and families.
- Start with low-cost pilots, not monuments.
- Prove one loop before expanding.

**Visual:** Frankston -> places -> tools -> people -> new creators -> stronger Frankston.

**Next obvious step:** choose the smallest test that proves the loop.

---

## Slide 4 - Core Idea: Creativity as Infrastructure

**Headline:** The ecosystem is the artwork.

**On-slide points**
- The artwork is not only an object; it is the ability a place builds.
- Every creative prompt should create a real-world action.
- Every pilot should leave behind a repeatable method.

**Visual:** Ecosystem map with schools, libraries, public art, artists, families, and council as practical nodes.

**Next obvious step:** decide which part of the ecosystem should host Stage One.

---

## Slide 5 - How the System Works

**Headline:** Screen as doorway, not destination.

**On-slide points**
- QR / Link -> Short Prompt -> Offline Action -> Optional Reflection -> Finish.
- No infinite scroll, no login by default, no child personal data.
- Create, then leave the screen.

**Visual:** Screen as Doorway diagram.

**Next obvious step:** require the proof checklist before any public QR or web layer.

---

## Slide 6 - Pilot Menu

**Headline:** Five options. One first test.

**On-slide points**
- Library Creative Pack.
- Living Mural QR Layer.
- Healthy Tech Mini-App.
- Frankston x Susono Classroom Pack.
- Respect Through Participation pathway.

**Visual:** Pilot Decision Sheet comparison.

**Next obvious step:** do not bundle the five. Pick one safest first pilot.

---

## Slide 7 - Recommended First Pilot

**Headline:** Recommended first pick: Library Creative Pack.

**On-slide points**
- Print-first, adult-supported, no-login, no-child-data.
- Low permission load and clear library/community use case.
- Strong platform for later mural or web-layer pilots.

**Visual:** One-page pilot card with 30/60/90 path.

**Next obvious step:** Tom confirms whether Library Creative Pack is the Stage One pilot candidate.

---

## Slide 8 - Healthy Technology Safeguards

**Headline:** Technology should give attention back.

**On-slide points**
- No dark patterns, gambling mechanics, infinite scroll, or default login.
- Short sessions, clear endings, accessibility basics, printable fallback.
- Anonymous aggregate metrics only if needed.

**Visual:** Healthy Technology checklist.

**Next obvious step:** test the pilot against no-login, no-child-data, accessibility, print fallback, and clear exit criteria.

---

## Slide 9 - First Nations Protocol Pathway

**Headline:** Protocol first. No permission implied.

**On-slide points**
- Do not generate language, symbols, stories, names, or cultural claims.
- Build process only until guided by appropriate Traditional Owner organisations.
- Watermark/status language stays visible: protocol pathway only - no permission implied.

**Visual:** First Nations protocol pathway diagram, process-only.

**Next obvious step:** keep this as a protocol task, not a content task.

---

## Slide 10 - Risk Controls

**Headline:** The proposal is strongest when it names its limits.

**On-slide points**
- No fake partnerships or implied approvals.
- No child data, no attention-capture design.
- No public release before proof, rights, and protocol review.

**Visual:** Three risk gates: protocol / child safety / evidence.

**Next obvious step:** review Risk Register before showing external material.

## Slide 11 - Stage One Council Ask
