## Pilot approval checklist

Use before launch:

- Purpose can be explained in one sentence.
- User group is defined.
- Frankston is the hero.
- B$S is presented as delivery engine or maker stamp only.
- Works without login wherever possible.
- Does not collect personal data from children.
- Does not use infinite scroll.
- Does not use dark patterns.
- Does not use gambling mechanics.
- Does not use streaks, loot boxes, or compulsive reward loops.
- Includes short-session design.
- Includes a clear ending.
- Includes an offline creative prompt.
- Includes family/classroom mode where relevant.
- Includes print-friendly version where useful.
- Includes accessibility basics.
- Includes plain privacy note.
- Uses anonymous aggregate metrics only where needed.
- Permission requirements are identified.
- Cultural protocol questions are escalated before content is created.
- Sister City content is exchange-based, not decorative.
- Stop/redesign conditions are written down.

## Questions to answer before Council sees a pilot

1. What is the public value?
2. Why does this need a digital layer?
3. What happens after the user leaves the screen?
4. Does it involve children?
5. Does it collect any personal data?
6. How can a teacher run it safely?
7. How can a library host it simply?
8. What can a family do without signing in?
9. How will Council know whether it worked?
10. What would make Council stop or redesign it?

- Feedback on the pilot approval form.

## What not to ask for yet

- City-wide rollout.
- Large platform funding.
- Child account system.
- Open submission portal.
- Complex app build.
- Permanent public infrastructure.
- Cultural content approval without an appropriate engagement pathway.

## Definition of ready

The 07 Healthy Technology section is ready for public pilot use when:

- the charter is reviewed
- the pilot approval form exists
- the first pilot passes the checklist
- privacy wording is plain
- no child personal data is collected
- offline prompt and print version are available
- accessibility basics are checked
- permissions are known
- success and failure measures are agreed
