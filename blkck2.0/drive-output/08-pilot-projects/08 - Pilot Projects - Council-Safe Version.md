# 08 - Pilot Projects
## Council-Safe Version

## Summary

Frankston 2035 - Art That Lives can be tested through one small pilot before any larger decision is considered.

The recommended approach is to select one pilot option, prepare a short scope, review permissions and risks, and decide whether a limited test is appropriate.

## Pilot options

1. **Living Mural / QR Public Art Layer**  
   A single existing artwork or public location is paired with a no-login web page and a short learning activity.

2. **Library Creative Pack**  
   A printable and digital pack for families, children, and teachers, with optional library programming.

3. **Frankston x Susono Classroom Exchange Pack**  
   A proposed classroom resource exploring sister-city learning. This would require review before any official exchange or public claim.

4. **Healthy Technology Mini-App**  
   A short-session creative web activity designed with no login, no personal data, no infinite scroll, and offline prompts.

5. **Respect Through Participation Pathway**  
   A process pilot to identify the appropriate pathway for any future First Nations learning work. This is not a cultural content pilot.

## Safeguards

- One pilot only.
- No long-term commitment implied.
- No personal data collected from children.
- No official council, school, Susono, or First Nations endorsement implied unless confirmed.
- Cultural content does not proceed without appropriate permission and guidance.
- Public material remains clearly labelled as proposal or pilot material until reviewed.

## Recommended first step

Council may wish to nominate one officer or team to review whether the Living Mural / QR Public Art Layer is suitable for a small scoped pilot.

If a public-art site is not practical, the Library Creative Pack is the lowest-friction alternative.
