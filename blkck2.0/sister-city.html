Sister City

Frankston ↔ Susono, as real exchange.

A proposed pathway for youth creativity, song, art, bilingual learning, and shared place stories between the bay and Mt Fuji.

Important status

Proposed exchange only until confirmed.

This page does not claim official Susono participation, school involvement, funding, council approval, or cultural approval. It describes a safe concept that would need guidance from the existing sister-city custodians before public use.

Exchange formats

Not decoration. A replyable creative bridge.

Song exchange

A simple chorus, rhythm, or class chant that can be replied to, adapted, translated, or performed with permission.

Art exchange

Poster prompts, student artworks, and class displays that invite a response rather than using another place as decoration.

Bilingual learning

Language-learning prompts only after review. Do not invent translations or cultural claims.

Mt Fuji / bay framing

Two landscapes as learning anchors: mountain, water, weather, memory, travel, and care for place.

Youth exchange

Class-to-class creative prompts where students make something, ask a question, and receive a response.

Public display

Library or school displays only after permissions, consent, translations, and partner review are complete.

Protocol for exchange

The relationship matters more than the artifact.

  1. Ask the existing sister-city custodians what a respectful first exchange could be.
  2. Keep the first artifact small: one poster, one song prompt, one class exchange, or one display.
  3. Confirm language review, image rights, student consent, and school policy before public sharing.
  4. Build for reply: students in one place should be able to respond to students in the other.
  5. Review the exchange before expanding.

Next obvious step

Prepare a one-page invitation brief for review by the correct sister-city relationship holders.

Until that review path exists, this remains a proposed exchange concept. It is not a launch announcement.
