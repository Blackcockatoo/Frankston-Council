Image Library

A clean holding system for Frankston visuals.

Use this gallery as the public asset map. These are placeholder visuals only; final images need rights, consent, captions, and approval before public use.

Categories

Ready for beach, pier, station, schools, nostalgia, public art, sister city, and community assets.

Visual status: placeholder graphics are safe to display. Real photographs, student work, public art images, community images, and exchange material remain pending permission.

Beach

Rights check required

Foreshore, light, water, walking, summer memory, weather.

Next: select one approved hero image and three supporting details with owner, date, caption, and usage note.

Pier

Rights check required

Arrival, lookout points, dusk, fishing, horizon, civic postcard moments.

Next: choose a rights-cleared wide image that reads quickly on mobile and add alt text.

Station

Rights check required

Movement, daily commute, youth routes, urban edges, wayfinding.

Next: use privacy-safe public context images and avoid identifiable close-ups unless consent exists.

Schools

Consent required

Classroom-safe images, student work examples, teacher pack visuals.

Next: use non-identifying process images first; confirm school policy and family consent before publication.

Nostalgia

Rights check required

Local memory, old postcards, family-safe archive items, then-and-now prompts.

Next: start with owned, licensed, or public-domain material and record source and copyright status.

Public Art

Artist/site permission

Murals, sculptures, walls, artist process, QR-ready sites.

Next: confirm artist credit, site-owner approval, and one candidate QR/story-trail location.

Sister City

Proposal only

Frankston and Susono exchange visuals, used only after review and permission.

Next: keep visuals as proposed exchange material until sister-city custodians confirm a review path.

Community

Consent required

Libraries, markets, makers, workshops, local families, public sessions.

Next: capture process shots before portraits and store consent/release notes with every public image.

Next obvious step

Create an approved image folder with captions, owner, permission status, and alt text for each image.

This page is intentionally useful before the assets are finished. It shows what is missing without faking a complete media library.

  • Use real Frankston photography where possible.
  • Do not publish student faces or community images without consent.
  • Do not imply First Nations or Susono approvals through imagery.
  • Record owner, location, date, caption, and usage permission.
