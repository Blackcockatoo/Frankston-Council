Where to start

What this is, who it's for, and what to do next.

Frankston 2035 is a public-facing proposal by Blue Snake Studio. It is not an official council program. It is a practical seed — a set of small, low-cost ideas for what public art, schools, libraries, and civic creativity could look like in Frankston.

The best place to start is the Library Creative Pack pilot. It needs no QR technology, no council approval process, and no budget over $500.

Plain English

What it is.

A civic proposal

Prepared by Blue Snake Studio. Not an official Frankston City Council publication. Not funded or endorsed unless formally agreed.

A practical seed

Five low-cost pilot ideas, ten printable poster seeds, a school pathway, a healthy technology charter, and a sister-city exchange proposal. All designed to be tested before being scaled.

A starting point

The strongest version of any pilot starts as a printable resource, a walk, or a classroom sheet — before any QR layer, app, or public launch is considered.

What it is not

Not an approved council program. Not an endorsed school partnership. Not a confirmed sister-city initiative. Not a funded grant. A proposal seed only, until formally approved.

Who it's for

Council, schools, libraries, artists, families.

Councillors and officers

Looking for a low-risk first move on public art, community creativity, or youth engagement. Start with the Library Creative Pack pilot brief.

Teachers and librarians

Looking for a practical classroom or community activity. Download the Lesson Pack Starter and the Student Artwork Pathway. No QR required.

Artists and community workers

Looking to connect with a civic pilot. Read the Living Mural Lesson Wall concept and the Frankston Creativity Trail brief.

Local families

Looking for something to do or notice in Frankston. Print a poster seed, take a walk, try the Scan Less / Notice More prompt with your kids.

Five minutes

What you can do right now.

  1. Read the Library Creative Pack pilot brief — plain English, one page.
  2. Download and print the one-page flyer to share at a meeting or community table.
  3. Print one poster seed and put it somewhere people will read it.
  4. Try the 30-minute lesson with a class or community group.
  5. Email Blue Snake Studio with one question or one idea.

Which pilot first

Start with the one that needs the least to begin.

The recommended first pilot is the Library Creative Pack. It needs a librarian, a table, twelve printed resources, and one 60-minute session. No QR technology. No council sign-off required. No budget over $500.

The second pilot — the Living Mural Lesson Wall — needs an existing artwork, artist permission, a weatherproof sign, and a printable worksheet. Still under $500. Still practical. A real place, a real lesson, a real wall.

Do not start with the sister-city exchange or the QR creativity trail. Both need relationships and permissions that take time to establish properly.
