# 08 - Pilot Projects
## Frankston 2035 - Art That Lives
### Main Document Draft

## Purpose

This document gives Council, teachers, libraries, community partners, artists, funders, and Blue Snake Studio a concrete menu of low-risk pilot options.

The project does not need a grand launch. It needs one small test that proves whether the idea can survive real-world conditions: permissions, site constraints, classroom usefulness, cultural protocol, privacy, maintenance, and public understanding.

---

## Pilot selection rule

Choose one pilot first.

Do not bundle all five into a first ask. Bundling makes the proposal easier to reject. A single pilot makes the proposal easier to test.

---

## Cost bands

No made-up dollar amounts are used in this document.

| Band | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Tiny | Mostly planning, copy, printable material, and one lightweight prototype. |
| Small | Needs simple production, limited design, web layer, printing, or facilitation. |
| Medium | Needs multiple partners, approvals, facilitation, or more careful review before launch. |

## Pilot 1 - Living Mural / QR Public Art Layer

### Purpose

Test whether one existing mural, foreshore artwork, or public location can carry a useful digital learning layer without turning the artwork into a gimmick.

### What it looks like

One approved public artwork or location gets a small QR marker. The QR opens a no-login web layer with:

- a plain-English explanation of the artwork/location
- a short learning activity
- a visual prompt or pattern exercise
- an optional classroom extension
- an offline action: sketch, notice, discuss, or make

The web layer collects no personal data. If usage is measured, it is anonymous aggregate usage only.

### Who it serves

- Councillors who need a visible, low-risk test
- Council arts/place teams
- Teachers and school groups
- Families and walkers
- Artists whose work can gain a second life

### What already exists that reduces risk

- Blue Snake Studio has built static/no-login web experiences.
- Existing BSS projects demonstrate pattern, maths, puzzle, and child-safe learning logic.
- Frankston already has public art and foreshore/civic locations.
- The pilot can be tested on one site without implying a city-wide rollout.

### What Council would need to provide

- Candidate artwork/location advice
- Site/signage permission pathway
- Any artist/copyright approval guidance
- Communications/privacy/accessibility expectations
- One point of contact for feasibility review

### What Blue Snake Studio would provide

- Prototype no-login web layer
- Draft learning activity
- QR landing page
- Printable teacher or visitor sheet
- Draft signage copy
- Anonymous aggregate usage summary, if approved

### Cost band

Small.

### 30 / 60 / 90 day timeline

| Time | Work |
|---|---|
| 30 days | Select candidate site, confirm permissions pathway, draft one-page pilot brief. |
| 60 days | Build prototype web layer, draft QR/signage copy, prepare teacher/visitor activity. |
| 90 days | Run limited test, collect anonymous usage and qualitative feedback, decide whether to continue, revise, or stop. |

### Risks

- Artist permission not secured
- QR signage clutter or vandalism
- Web layer feels gimmicky
- Accessibility gaps
- Public confusion about council endorsement

### Safeguards

- Use one site only
- Label as pilot/proposal if public
- No login and no personal data
- No First Nations or sister-city content unless separately approved
- Include non-QR alternative text or printable access

### Success metrics

- Anonymous scan count
- Teacher/visitor feedback
- Completion/use of printable activity
- Site owner/artist feedback
- Clear decision after 90 days

### Next decision required

Nominate one candidate artwork or location for feasibility review.

### What done looks like

One real location has one working learning layer, one printable activity, one usage/feedback summary, and a go/stop/revise recommendation.

## Pilot 2 - Library Creative Pack

### Purpose

Test whether a library-based printable/digital pack can help families, children, and teachers make something connected to Frankston's creative culture.

### What it looks like

A small pack available at a library desk, event table, or downloadable page:

- printable colouring/making sheets
- a short making prompt
- a simple song or audio layer
- QR links to safe no-login pages
- one family/classroom activity
- optional library event format

### Who it serves

- Libraries
- Families
- Teachers
- Children
- Community workers
- Local artists looking for low-barrier participation

### What already exists that reduces risk

- Blue Snake Studio has child-safe static website and printable-pack experience.
- Nara's Colour Quest is a relevant precedent for simple, private, child-facing material.
- Libraries are natural low-risk settings for printable resources.

### What Council would need to provide

- Library contact
- Advice on event/program fit
- Printing constraints
- Child-safety and accessibility expectations
- Review of public copy

### What Blue Snake Studio would provide

- Printable pack
- Web/QR landing page
- Short audio/song layer if suitable
- Facilitator notes
- Feedback form

### Cost band

Tiny to small.

### 30 / 60 / 90 day timeline

| Time | Work |
|---|---|
| 30 days | Select library use case, draft pack outline, identify review needs. |
| 60 days | Produce printable draft, QR page, and facilitator sheet. |
| 90 days | Trial at one library table/event or with one small user group; collect feedback. |

### Risks

- Pack feels like generic kids' activity
- Printing or staffing burden
- Audio rights unclear
- QR links become stale
- Public content not reviewed

### Safeguards

- Low-prep design
- Print-first, QR-second
- No child data collection
- Clear adult-controlled publishing rules
- Rights check for images/audio

### Success metrics

- Packs taken/downloaded
- Library staff feedback
- Family/teacher feedback
- Repeat-use interest
- Number of completed activities returned or shown voluntarily

### Next decision required

Choose one library or library-adjacent setting for a small pack trial.

### What done looks like

One pack exists, one library/family setting tests it, and feedback shows whether it is useful enough to improve.

## Pilot 3 - Frankston x Susono Classroom Exchange Pack

### Purpose

Test whether the Frankston-Susono sister-city relationship can become a simple classroom resource without pretending an official exchange has already been approved.

### What it looks like

A teacher-friendly starter pack:

- bilingual English/Japanese starter phrases
- pronunciation support
- song/chant/rhythm layer
- student artwork or poster response concept
- simple "send/reply" exchange structure
- teacher notes that clearly label the pack as a proposal until reviewed

No official Susono participation is claimed unless confirmed.

### Who it serves

- Teachers
- Students
- Sister-city relationship custodians
- Council community/international staff
- Families interested in cultural exchange

### What already exists that reduces risk

- Frankston has a real sister-city relationship with Susono.
- Blue Snake Studio has existing Frankston-Susono creative material.
- The concept can begin as a review-ready classroom prototype before any public exchange.

### What Council would need to provide

- Correct sister-city review contact
- Advice on what is appropriate to propose
- School/community pathway guidance
- Review of any public language

### What Blue Snake Studio would provide

- Draft classroom pack
- Song/chant layer
- Poster/artwork response template
- QR page
- Teacher notes
- Clear "proposal pending review" language

### Cost band

Small to medium, depending on review and translation needs.

### 30 / 60 / 90 day timeline

| Time | Work |
|---|---|
| 30 days | Prepare a one-page invitation/review brief for sister-city custodians. |
| 60 days | Draft starter pack only if review path is open; check language and claims. |
| 90 days | Run a limited classroom review or paper prototype; do not publish as official exchange unless approved. |

### Risks

- Implied official Susono participation
- Translation or pronunciation errors
- Cultural flattening
- Teachers receive a resource with too much setup
- Student work rights/consent unclear

### Safeguards

- Proposal language only
- Review by relationship custodians
- Teacher simplicity test
- Translation checking
- Consent process for any shared student work

### Success metrics

- Review feedback from appropriate contacts
- Teacher clarity rating
- Student activity completion in a controlled setting
- Number of corrections logged
- Decision on whether a real exchange is welcome

### Next decision required

Identify who can review a Frankston-Susono classroom exchange proposal.

### What done looks like

The pack is either approved for a small exchange path, revised, or parked. It does not drift into public claims without review.

## Pilot 4 - Healthy Technology Mini-App

### Purpose

Test a child-safe creative web app that gives attention back to real places, paper, conversation, and making.

### What it looks like

A short-session, no-login web app:

- one creative prompt
- one simple interaction
- no infinite scroll
- no dark patterns
- no ads
- no personal data
- no public comments
- prompts to make something offline
- family/classroom mode
- clear finish point

### Who it serves

- Children
- Families
- Teachers
- Libraries
- Council staff concerned with digital wellbeing
- Funders looking for child-safe innovation

### What already exists that reduces risk

- Blue Snake Studio has built static/no-login child-facing sites.
- Nara's Colour Quest provides a child-safe design precedent.
- Existing BSS logic/puzzle projects show the studio can build simple interactive systems.

### What Council would need to provide

- Review expectations for child safety and privacy
- Potential school/library test context
- Accessibility expectations
- Communications guidance if publicly trialled

### What Blue Snake Studio would provide

- Static prototype
- Privacy note
- Short-session interaction
- Offline prompt sheet
- Family/classroom mode
- Feedback form

### Cost band

Small.

### 30 / 60 / 90 day timeline

| Time | Work |
|---|---|
| 30 days | Define one use case and child-safety checklist. |
| 60 days | Build static prototype and offline companion sheet. |
| 90 days | Test with a small family/classroom/library group; review privacy, accessibility, and usefulness. |

### Risks

- "Healthy technology" claim not backed by design
- App becomes sticky or distracting
- Privacy concern from parents/schools
- Accessibility gaps
- Maintenance burden

### Safeguards

- No login
- No personal data
- No infinite scroll
- No engagement-time metric
- Clear end state
- Offline making prompt
- Adult-controlled use context

### Success metrics

- Short completion rate
- Offline activity completion
- Parent/teacher feedback
- Accessibility review notes
- Evidence that the app reduces, not increases, attention capture

### Next decision required

Choose one simple creative interaction and one safe test context.

### What done looks like

One mini-app has been tested, reviewed, and either improved, stopped, or connected to a library/classroom pilot.

## Pilot 5 - Respect Through Participation Pathway

### Purpose

Create a protocol-first pathway for future First Nations learning work. This is not a cultural content pilot unless permission exists.

### What it looks like

A process pilot:

- map the correct engagement pathway
- identify appropriate Traditional Owner organisations or council protocol contacts
- prepare questions, not content
- hold listening sessions only if invited
- document what should not be done
- identify possible future outputs only after guidance

No invented language, symbols, stories, histories, or cultural claims.

### Who it serves

- Traditional Owner organisations
- Council staff
- Community educators
- Blue Snake Studio
- Future participants who need a respectful path

### What already exists that reduces risk

- The foundation documents already state the permission-first rule.
- The proposal has not claimed First Nations partnership.
- This pilot can be framed as governance and listening, not content production.

### What Council would need to provide

- Protocol guidance
- Advice on the correct contact pathway
- Any existing council policy or engagement expectations
- Clarity on what should not be approached yet

### What Blue Snake Studio would provide

- Draft engagement brief
- Listening questions
- Documentation of boundaries
- Revised project language based on guidance
- No cultural content unless invited and guided

### Cost band

Medium, because proper engagement needs time, care, and potentially paid participation.

### 30 / 60 / 90 day timeline

| Time | Work |
|---|---|
| 30 days | Prepare internal protocol brief and identify correct guidance pathway. |
| 60 days | Seek advice on whether engagement is appropriate and what form it should take. |
| 90 days | If invited, hold listening step or revise/park the pathway. No public cultural output is expected. |

### Risks

- Tokenism
- Extractive consultation
- Implied permission
- Under-budgeted engagement
- Treating acknowledgement as participation

### Safeguards

- Process pilot only
- No content production by default
- Right to say no, pause, redirect, or stop
- Paid/properly supported engagement if it proceeds
- Public language reviewed before use

### Success metrics

- Correct engagement pathway identified
- Guidance received, or respectful decision to pause
- Project language improved
- Clear list of prohibited/unsafe assumptions
- Future output criteria defined by the right people

### Next decision required

Ask Council or appropriate advisors what the correct Traditional Owner engagement pathway is. Do not guess.

### What done looks like

The project has a safer protocol pathway and either an invitation to continue, a request to pause, or clear guidance on what must happen before any content exists.

## Pilot comparison

| Pilot | Best first audience | Cost band | Main risk | Best use |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Living Mural / QR Public Art Layer | Council arts/place team | Small | Permissions and public clarity | Visible first test |
| Library Creative Pack | Libraries/families | Tiny-small | Generic content / staffing burden | Low-risk participation |
| Frankston x Susono Classroom Exchange Pack | Teachers / sister-city custodians | Small-medium | Overclaiming official participation | Review-ready exchange proposal |
| Healthy Technology Mini-App | Teachers / families | Small | Privacy and screen-time concerns | Child-safe digital proof |
| Respect Through Participation Pathway | Council / Traditional Owner pathway | Medium | Cultural protocol breach | Trust and governance |

---

## Recommended Stage One candidate

Recommended first candidate: **Living Mural / QR Public Art Layer**.

Reason: it is the easiest pilot to explain, the easiest to keep small, and the clearest expression of the core thesis: public art that teaches.

The Library Creative Pack is the best backup if outdoor signage/site permissions are too slow.
