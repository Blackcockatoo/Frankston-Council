Sister City

Frankston ↔ Susono, as real exchange.

A proposed pathway for youth creativity, song, art, bilingual learning, and shared place stories between the bay and Mt Fuji.

Proposed exchange only. This page does not claim official Susono participation, school involvement, funding, council approval, or cultural approval.

Proposed, not announced. No official program exists yet. This is a scoped concept awaiting review.
Exchange means reply. Not a display of one culture to another. A message that receives an answer.
Review before release. Language, images, student work, and cultural references all need partner and family review first.
Relationship first. The relationship between Frankston and Susono matters more than any single artifact.

Exchange formats

Not decoration. A replyable creative bridge.

Song

Song exchange

A simple chorus, rhythm, or class chant that can be replied to, adapted, translated, or performed — with partner review and permission.

Art

Art exchange

Poster prompts, student artworks, and class displays that invite a response rather than using another culture as decoration.

Language

Bilingual learning

Language-learning prompts only after review by qualified speakers. No invented translations or cultural claims.

Place

Bay and mountain

Two landscapes as learning anchors: water, weather, memory, travel, and care for place — shared without overclaiming.

Exchange protocol

The relationship matters more than the artifact.

  1. Ask the existing sister-city custodians what a respectful first exchange could be.
  2. Keep the first artifact small: one poster, one song prompt, one class exchange, or one display.
  3. Confirm language review, image rights, student consent, and school policy before any public sharing.
  4. Build for reply: students in one place should be able to respond to students in the other.
  5. Review the exchange before expanding. If it does not feel right to either community, stop or adapt.

What not to do

Common risks in sister-city creative projects.

Tokenism. Using another culture's imagery, language, or traditions as decoration rather than engaging with real people.

Translation errors. Generating Japanese or any other language without qualified speaker review and community consent.

Uneven participation. Creating material that only benefits one side of the exchange.

Announcing before confirming. Publicly naming Susono as a partner before the existing relationship holders have reviewed and agreed to participation.

Implying official program. This is a proposed concept until the correct review pathway confirms it.
