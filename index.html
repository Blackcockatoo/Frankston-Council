What it is

A framework for public art that keeps working after the paint dries.

Frankston 2035 - Art That Lives turns existing public art, foreshore walks, libraries, classrooms, and sister-city relationships into a living creative system. A mural can become a lesson. A trail can become a noticing exercise. A poster can become a prompt for a child, teacher, artist, or resident to add something back.

This is not a monument plan. It is a small-pilot framework for building makers. The first move should be cheap, visible, testable, and easy to stop or improve.

Why Frankston

The Four Loops

The ecosystem is the artwork.

1

Wall to lesson

Public art becomes a worksheet, browser activity, classroom prompt, or local maths and pattern exercise.

2

Walk to attention

3

Sister-city material becomes a replyable object: a song, poster, class pack, or bilingual learning prompt with review.

4

Viewer to maker

Residents and students add work back into the system through guided, moderated, low-risk contribution pathways.

Healthy Technology

Technology should give attention back to real places and real people.

The digital layer is a doorway, not the destination. It should be static or lightweight where possible, private by design, and measured in ways that do not reward keeping children on screens.

No infinite scroll. No dark patterns. No gambling mechanics. No child data by default. A good scan says: look at this wall, ask this question, try this activity, talk to someone beside you, then put the device away.

Cultural Protocol

First Nations content begins with permission.

No language, symbols, stories, names, or claims are invented here. Any future cultural content must be guided by appropriate Traditional Owner organisations, with the right to say no, pause, edit, or remove.

Next step: prepare an invitation brief for review, not a finished program announcement.

Sister City

Exchange means reply.

Frankston-Susono content should focus on real shared artifacts: class posters, song responses, language practice, and approved learning packs. The goal is exchange, not decoration.

Next step: ask the existing sister-city custodians what a small respectful first exchange could be.
