# 01 — Executive Summary
## Visual Layout Suggestions

---

**Page 1 — Cover / Hero**
- Memetic primary line ("A mural is seen once. A living mural keeps teaching.") set large in serif display, centred, on `paper` background.
- Small project lockup beneath: "Frankston 2035 — Art That Lives" in sans, `bay-mid`.
- No imagery on this page — let the line do the work, per Design Principle 1 ("one page, one message").

**Page 2 — "Frankston, 2035" scene-setting**
- Full-bleed real Frankston foreshore photography (per Asset Standards) as a quiet background wash, `bay-deep` gradient overlay at the bottom third for text legibility.
- The three "picture this" vignettes (mural-as-lesson, bilingual trail, library-pack-to-wall) as three short captioned blocks — icons only, no stock photography of people.

**Page 3 — Why this matters now**
- Four-icon row: public art / education / Susono / First Nations — each a simple line icon in `bay-mid`, captioned one line each.
- Pull-quote treatment for "These are usually four separate council line items. This proposal treats them as one opportunity."

**Page 4 — What's proposed + Low-cost pilot pathway**
- Left column: prose, What's Proposed.
- Right column: a contained "pathway card" — `sand` background panel listing the five pilot bullet facts (one location / one term / one budget / zero ongoing / zero data), each with a small checkmark-style icon. This is the page most likely to be screenshotted — it should stand alone.

**Page 5 — What Council can do next**
- Three numbered steps as a simple horizontal 1-2-3 diagram, `bay-mid` circles, `bay-pale` connecting line, no "you are here" coral marker needed here (nothing is mid-process yet).

**Page 6 — Bridge to Section 04**
- Closing pull-quote: "The technology isn't a pitch. It's already built."
- Single coral-accented call-to-action box: "See Section 04 — Blue Snake Studio" with a small forward arrow — this page exists specifically to hand off into the credibility document, so it should feel like a page-turn, not an ending.

**Don'ts specific to this document:**
- Don't open with a Blue Snake Studio logo or branding — per the brief, Frankston is the hero. The B$S credit stays in the footer only (per Branding Rules, Section 8).
- Don't let the low-cost pathway page get visually buried under prose — it's the page that overcomes the "this will cost a fortune" objection and deserves the most generous white space in the document.
