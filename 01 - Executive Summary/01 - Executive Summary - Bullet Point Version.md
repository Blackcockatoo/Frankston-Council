# 01 — Executive Summary
## Bullet-Point Version

---

**The vision**
- Public art that teaches, travels, and can be added to — not just looked at once
- A foreshore mural a class can scan and turn into a maths lesson
- A trail told in English and Japanese, giving the Susono relationship a place to live
- A library pack where students build art the city actually displays

**Why now**
- Frankston has already invested in public art and placemaking — but most of it is seen once, then becomes wallpaper
- Schools want safe, local, genuinely engaging digital learning tools
- The Susono sister-city relationship is real but under-resourced with shared content
- Growing appetite for respectful, consent-based First Nations involvement in public space
- Four separate priorities — one shared opportunity

**What's proposed**
- A staged program adding an interactive layer to existing public art
- Starts with one small pilot, not a big commitment
- Built on technology Blue Snake Studio has already designed and tested (Section 04)
- Council funds an application, not an invention

**The low-cost pilot pathway**
- 1 location · 1 school term · 1 modest one-off budget
- $0 ongoing cost beyond the pilot
- $0 / zero personal data collected from children — anonymous, aggregate usage only

**What Council can do next**
1. Note the proposal, signal appetite for Stage One
2. Nominate a candidate pilot location
3. Request the Full Proposal + Pilot Project Sheets for a costed scope

**The proof behind it**
- Section 04: Blue Snake Studio's existing, working portfolio
- Already-shipped tools, tested logic engines, original maths systems
- Nothing speculative — Council is pointing a working lab at a civic purpose
