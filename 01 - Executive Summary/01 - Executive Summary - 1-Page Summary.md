# 01 — Executive Summary
## 1-Page Summary

---

**The picture:** A Year 4 class scans a code beside a foreshore mural and the wall becomes a maths lesson. A trail tells its story in English and Japanese, giving Frankston's Susono sister-city relationship somewhere to actually live. A class builds a piece of public art and sees it added to a wall the whole city walks past.

**Why now:** Frankston has already invested in public art, foreshore activation, and a real sister-city relationship — but a mural is usually seen once, then becomes wallpaper. Schools want safe, local digital learning tools. There's genuine appetite for respectful First Nations involvement in public space. These are usually four separate council priorities. This proposal treats them as one opportunity.

**What's proposed:** A staged program adding an interactive, educational layer to Frankston's existing public art — starting with one small, low-cost pilot. The technology already exists, built and tested by Blue Snake Studio, a Frankston-based studio (see Section 04). Council isn't funding invention — it's pointing an existing capability at a civic purpose.

**The low-cost pilot pathway:**
- One location · one school term · one modest one-off budget
- Zero ongoing cost commitment beyond the pilot
- Zero data collected from children beyond anonymous, aggregate usage

**What Council can do next:**
1. Note this proposal and indicate appetite for a Stage One pilot
2. Nominate a candidate location
3. Request the Full Proposal and Pilot Project Sheets for a costed scope

**The proof:** Section 04 — Blue Snake Studio — sets out the studio's existing, working creative-technology projects. Nothing here asks Council to fund an idea. It asks Council to use a lab that's already running.
