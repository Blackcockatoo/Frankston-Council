# Council Ask - Stage One Pilot Decision
## Frankston 2035 - Art That Lives

## The ask in one sentence

Approve exploration of one small, scoped Stage One pilot that tests whether Frankston's existing public art and creative culture can become a useful learning and participation layer.

This is not a request to fund the whole vision.

---

## What exactly are we asking Council to do?

We are asking Council to allow a modest scoping conversation for one pilot.

The safe version is:

1. Identify one suitable pilot pathway.
2. Nominate one council contact or team to test feasibility.
3. Confirm what approvals, site constraints, and risk checks would be needed.
4. Decide whether a small pilot brief should be prepared.

No long-term commitment is required at this stage.

---

## What are we not asking Council to do?

- We are not asking Council to approve the full Frankston 2035 vision.
- We are not asking Council to adopt Blue Snake Studio as an exclusive provider.
- We are not asking Council to approve First Nations content.
- We are not asking Council to approve Susono-facing content.
- We are not asking Council to commit to a multi-year budget.
- We are not asking Council to launch a public platform.
- We are not asking Council to use official branding on draft material.

---

## What decision is needed now?

The only decision needed now is whether Council is open to exploring one small pilot.

The decision can be as cautious as:

> "Proceed to a scoped pilot brief for one candidate location or program area, subject to normal council review."

---

## What can wait?

- Final pilot location.
- Final budget.
- Procurement pathway.
- Public launch.
- School participation.
- Library programming.
- First Nations content.
- Sister-city exchange content.
- Full design package.
- QR code production.
- Ongoing maintenance model.

Those decisions should wait until the first pilot brief is real.

---

## What would a safe yes look like?

A safe yes would not approve the whole program. It would approve a bounded scoping step:

| Item | Safe yes |
|---|---|
| Scope | One pilot only. |
| Duration | 30/60/90-day planning and test pathway. |
| Council role | Nominate a contact, site, or program area for feasibility review. |
| Studio role | Prepare a pilot brief, prototype, and risk controls. |
| Public claim | "Proposal under exploration," not "Council program." |
| Data | Anonymous aggregate usage only, if any data is collected. |
| Cultural content | Excluded unless appropriate permissions and guidance exist. |

---

## What would a cautious next step look like?

1. Council officer reviews the pilot options.
2. One option is selected for scoping.
3. Blue Snake Studio prepares a one-page pilot brief.
4. Council checks site, permissions, procurement, privacy, and communications risks.
5. A go/no-go decision is made before any public launch.

---

## Minimum viable pilot

The minimum viable pilot is one of these:

- One existing public artwork with one QR-linked no-login learning layer.
- One library creative pack with printable sheets and a simple QR/audio layer.
- One classroom exchange starter pack held as proposal material until sister-city review.
- One healthy technology mini-app tested privately with family/classroom feedback.
- One First Nations process pilot focused on listening and protocol, not content creation.

The strongest first candidate is likely the Living Mural / QR Public Art Layer because it is concrete, visible, and easy to keep small.

---

## Useful support besides money

Council support does not need to begin with funding. Useful non-financial support includes:

- A nominated officer contact.
- Advice on a suitable pilot site or program area.
- Advice on temporary signage rules.
- Advice on privacy, accessibility, and child-safety expectations.
- Introduction to library, arts, youth, or education staff.
- Guidance on what not to touch yet.
- A clear review pathway for draft public material.

## Council departments that might logically be involved

| Area | Why |
|---|---|
| Arts and Culture / Creative City | Public art, artists, creative placemaking. |
| Libraries | Printable packs, family sessions, community learning. |
| Community Development | Participation pathways and local partner links. |
| Youth / Families / Education liaison | Child-safe learning activities and school relevance. |
| Digital / Communications | QR/web layer, public messaging, accessibility. |
| Governance / Procurement | Safe engagement pathway and conflicts/risk checks. |
| Place / Foreshore / City activation | Site suitability for any trail or wall-based pilot. |

| Sister-city / International relationships custodian | Only for review of any Frankston-Susono exchange proposal. |

---

## Recommended first wording

> Blue Snake Studio is seeking Council's feedback on whether one small, scoped pilot could be explored under the Frankston 2035 - Art That Lives framework. The first step would not commit Council to a program or budget. It would simply identify whether one public artwork, library setting, or learning context is suitable for a modest pilot brief.

---

## What "yes" should not mean

Yes should not mean:

- "Build the whole platform."
- "Use council branding."
- "Announce a partnership."
- "Begin First Nations content."
- "Launch to schools."
- "Invite public submissions."

Those are later decisions.
