## Main Document Draft

### The engine behind the proposal

Section 01 described what Frankston could become. This section answers the practical question every careful reader should ask next: is there really something behind this, or is it just a pitch?

Blue Snake Studio is a Frankston-based creative-technology studio. Over the past two years it has built and shipped a working body of interactive, educational, and creative-technology projects — independently of this proposal, for its own sake. That existing body of work is the engine this proposal asks Council to point at a civic purpose. Frankston doesn't need to fund an idea. It needs to redirect a lab that's already running.

### What already exists

**Digital DNA Laboratory** — an interactive web experience teaching pattern, symmetry, and mathematics through five distinct modes of play: a helix explorer, a mandala painter, a particle field, a generative sound temple, and a guided journey mode. Runs in any browser. No install, no login, no data collection. This is the closest existing analogue to what a "living mural" interaction could feel like for a Frankston pilot — proof the interaction pattern already works.

**Nara's Colour Quest** — a fully static, kid-safe colour-and-pattern learning website, built under the "Blue Snake Studios Junior" sub-brand specifically to demonstrate that child-facing tools can be simple, private, and safe by design rather than by policy add-on. This is the direct technical precedent for any Teacher Kit or Library Pack content under this proposal.

**Hepta Lock** — a deterministic, reversible logic puzzle engine: a seven-node "lock" game, tested end-to-end with a verified hint system. This demonstrates the studio can ship rigorously tested interactive logic, not just visual decoration — relevant wherever a pilot needs an interaction to be genuinely correct, not just pretty.

**Moss60** — an original mathematical system: base-60 number theory built from the prime sequence 2, 3, 5, 7. It underlies all of the above. It is real, working number theory, translated into something playable. This is the depth behind the studio's "maths from a mural" claim in Section 01 — it isn't a metaphor, it's a working system already capable of generating that kind of content.

**The Black Wing Crew / Frankston–Susono cultural exchange** — an existing creative project connecting Frankston to its Japanese sister city, Susono, through language, music, and story. This is the real relationship Section 05 (Frankston × Susono) proposes building on — not a hypothetical bridge, an existing one.

### What this proves, specifically

Each existing project maps directly onto a capability the Frankston proposal depends on:

| Existing B$S work | What it proves for Frankston |
|---|---|
| Digital DNA Laboratory | Interactive, browser-based learning layers are already a solved problem for this studio |
| Nara's Colour Quest | Child-safe, privacy-first design is a default, not an afterthought |
| Hepta Lock | The studio ships tested, verified logic — not just visuals |
| Moss60 | The "art that teaches maths" idea has real mathematical substance behind it |
| Black Wing Crew / Susono exchange | The Frankston–Susono bridge already exists; this proposal extends it, doesn't invent it |

### What this is not

To be direct, consistent with the Constitution's tone rules: this is not a claim that any of the above projects were built *for* Frankston Council, and it is not a claim that they can be deployed unmodified into a council pilot. Every one of them would need genuine, scoped adaptation work to become a Frankston-specific pilot. What this section proves is technical capability and design philosophy — not a finished product sitting on a shelf waiting for a council sticker.

### Why this matters for risk

A studio with no shipped work asking Council to fund the invention of an interactive public-art platform is a real financial and reputational risk. A studio with a working, tested, two-year body of relevant projects asking Council to fund the *adaptation* of existing capability to a specific civic purpose is a materially smaller risk — the hard technical problems (browser-based interactivity, child privacy, tested logic, a real mathematical content engine) are already solved. What remains is application, not invention.

### The bridge back

None of this is offered as a portfolio for its own sake. Every project above exists to answer one question: can this actually be built? The answer is yes, because most of the hard parts already have been — and Section 01's low-cost pilot pathway is exactly the proportionate next step for proving that adaptation, not invention, is what Frankston is funding.
