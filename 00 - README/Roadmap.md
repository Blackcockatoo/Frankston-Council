## Phase 0 - Foundation

**Objective:** Lock the thesis, voice, claim rules, protocol boundaries, and folder operating system.

**Deliverables**
- `Project Vision.md`
- `Foundation (Chunk 00 - Constitution)/Chunk 00 - Project Foundation.md`
- `README.md`
- `Contents.md`
- `Roadmap.md`
- `Risk Register.md`
- `Asset Register.md`
- `Proof Register.md`
- `Design Production Queue.md`

**Dependencies**
- Tom confirms the source-of-truth hierarchy.
- No public claim is made until governance docs are aligned.

**Risks**
- The project looks more finished than it is.
- First Nations and Susono language gets treated as content instead of relationship work.
- Files multiply without ownership.

**Decision points**
- Which files are allowed to be shown externally?
- Which sections are blocked until human engagement happens?
- Which claims are proven enough to survive council scrutiny?

**Success criteria**
- A new contributor can understand the project in under 10 minutes.
- Every active folder has a purpose and next action.
- Risk, proof, and asset registers exist before public packaging expands.

## Phase 1 - Council-facing intro pack

**Objective:** Make the council ask clear, modest, and hard to misunderstand.

**Deliverables**
- `01 - Executive Summary` pack
- `04 - Blue Snake Studio` proof pack
- `Council Ask - Stage One Pilot Decision.md`
- `12-Slide Council Deck Scaffold.md`
- Hero Diagram
- Pilot Decision Sheet
- Proof Register

**Dependencies**
- Phase 0 source-of-truth docs.
- Real proof links/screenshots for Blue Snake Studio projects.
- A selected or shortlisted pilot from Phase 2.

**Risks**
- The ask becomes too large.
- Studio proof reads like ego-branding.
- Council readers cannot tell what decision is required.
- Draft/proposal claims sound like evidence.

**Decision points**
- Is the ask "approve exploration of one small scoped pilot"?
- Is the safest first pilot the Library Creative Pack?
- Who inside council would logically receive the first conversation?
- What support is needed besides money?

**Success criteria**
- A councillor can state the ask in one sentence.
- The document says what is not being asked.
- No claim implies council approval, funding, cultural approval, Susono participation, or partnership.

## Phase 2 - Pilot projects

**Objective:** Turn the vision into testable 30/60/90-day pilot options.

**Deliverables**
- `08 - Pilot Projects` full treatment
- `Pilot Menu one-pager`
- `Pilot Decision Sheet`
- 30/60/90 pilot timeline, still to build

**Dependencies**
- Council-facing ask.
- Blue Snake Studio proof assets.
- Human decision on which pilot is first.
- Protocol review for First Nations and sister-city pathways.

**Risks**
- Too many pilots are pitched at once.
- Cost and workload are implied but not scoped.
- Process-first cultural work is mistaken for content production.

**Decision points**
- Which single pilot is Stage One?
- What is the minimum viable pilot?
- What would "done" look like after 90 days?

**Success criteria**
- Each pilot has a purpose, timeline, risk controls, metrics, and next decision.
- At least one pilot can be explained without a deck.
- No pilot depends on unconfirmed cultural permission or official partner participation.

## Phase 3 - Design and visual packaging

**Objective:** Make the package skimmable, credible, printable, and shareable without outrunning the content.

**Deliverables**
- Updated static website / landing page
- Poster seed pack
- Screen as Doorway diagram
- Pilot Menu one-pager
- Hero Diagram / Frankston system map
- Pilot Decision Sheet
- Design Production Queue priority items
- Print-friendly PDFs, still to export
- QR landing-page draft structure, not started

**Dependencies**
- Stable Phase 1 and Phase 2 wording.
- Asset rights check.
- Accessibility and contrast review.
- Protocol review before public cultural references.

**Risks**
- Design makes draft material feel official.
- Visuals overpromise partnership or council endorsement.
- AI-generated assets introduce credibility or rights concerns.

**Decision points**
- Which assets can be public?
- What must carry a draft/proposal label?
- Is council branding allowed? If not, do not imply it.
- Which visual is the first PDF export candidate?

**Success criteria**
- A skimmer understands the idea in under 60 seconds.
- Every page points to a practical next step.
- Design supports the civic idea; it does not turn into studio mythology.

## Phase 4 - Council engagement

**Objective:** Move from internal proposal to a safe first conversation with council.

**Deliverables**
- Meeting brief
- Visual deck converted from the 12-slide scaffold
- One-page council ask
- Anticipated questions and honest answers
- Follow-up email template

**Dependencies**
- One selected Stage One pilot.
- Confirmed internal risk position.
- Council department mapping.
- Proof Register gaps reduced.

**Risks**
- Asking the wrong person first.
- Triggering procurement questions before the pilot is scoped.
- Looking like a request for endorsement rather than exploration.

**Decision points**
- Who is the first council contact?
- Is this a formal submission, informal briefing, or community proposal?
- What can be left out until asked?

**Success criteria**
- Council understands the next decision.
- No one is asked to approve the whole vision.
- Follow-up action is concrete: location, officer conversation, or pilot scoping.

## Phase 5 - Community partner pathway

**Objective:** Build participation without exploiting communities or creating unmanaged promises.

**Deliverables**
- Teacher pathway
- Library pathway
- Artist pathway
- Community partner invitation
- Traditional Owner engagement brief, if and only if appropriate human-led outreach is ready
- Sister-city exchange invitation brief, if and only if relationship custodians invite it

**Dependencies**
- Pilot choice.
- Council engagement feedback.
- Human-led protocol decisions.

**Risks**
- Treating community partners as decoration.
- Volunteer burnout.
- Inviting contributions without moderation, rights, or privacy rules.
- Cultural content being requested before trust exists.

**Decision points**
- Who is genuinely invited?
- What is the smallest useful role?
- What does each partner get back?

**Success criteria**
- Partners understand the commitment before saying yes.
- The first pilot creates useful learning, not unmanaged obligations.
- Protocol and consent remain visible.
