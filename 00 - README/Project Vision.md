# Project Vision
## Frankston 2035 — Art That Lives
### Blue Snake Studio × Frankston City Council

---

## The one sentence

**Frankston already has art on its walls. Blue Snake Studio's offer is to make that art teachable, interactive, and shared — so a mural, a trail, or a story isn't just seen once and forgotten, but becomes something a child can learn from, a family can create with, and a community can pass down.**

---

## Where this comes from

Blue Snake Studio is not a marketing agency pitching a concept. It is a working Frankston-based studio that has, over the past two years, already built a body of functioning creative-technology projects:

- **Digital DNA Laboratory** — an interactive web experience teaching pattern, symmetry and mathematics through five different modes of play (helix exploration, mandala painting, particle fields, generative sound, guided journeys), built for browsers, requiring no install, no login, no data collection.
- **Nara's Colour Quest** — a fully static, kid-safe colour-and-pattern learning website built under the "Blue Snake Studios Junior" sub-brand, designed from the ground up with child privacy and simplicity as first principles.
- **Hepta Lock** — a deterministic, reversible logic puzzle engine (a seven-node "lock" game), tested end-to-end, demonstrating that B$S can ship rigorously verified interactive logic, not just visual decoration.
- **Moss60** — an original mathematical system (base-60 number theory using the prime sequence 2-3-5-7) that underlies all of the above. It is real, working number theory translated into something a ten-year-old can play with.
- **The Black Wing Crew / Frankston–Susono cultural exchange** — an existing creative project connecting Frankston to its Japanese sister city, Susono, through language, music and story.

This matters because it changes the nature of the proposal. Council is not being asked to fund an idea. Council is being asked to **point an existing, working creative-technology practice at a civic and educational purpose** — Frankston's public space, Frankston's schools, Frankston's bicultural relationships, and Frankston's First Nations story.

---

## What Frankston has already built

Before describing what's new, it's worth naming what's already true, because the proposal is built **on top of** it, not instead of it:

- Frankston has invested in public art, foreshore activation, and creative placemaking across the municipality.
- Frankston holds a genuine, ongoing sister-city relationship with Susono, Japan — a real cultural bridge, not a hypothetical one.
- Frankston sits on Bunurong/Boonwurrung Country, with an existing and growing civic commitment to First Nations recognition.
- Frankston has an active education sector — primary schools, libraries, and community programs — already looking for ways to bring digital literacy and creative learning together.

Blue Snake Studio's role is not to introduce these things to Frankston. It's to **build the connective tissue** between them: technology that makes a mural teach, a trail tell a story in two languages, a library pack carry both, and a classroom safely create with all of it.

---

## The central idea, restated

> Art shouldn't stop on the wall. It should become something people can learn from, create with, and share — across generations, across cultures, and across the community.

A mural is a single moment, fixed in time, seen once by most people who walk past it. The proposal in this folder is about giving murals, trails, and public stories a second life: scannable, interactive, multilingual, and built with the same rigour B$S already applies to its number theory and its puzzle engines — not bolted-on gimmicks, but genuinely well-made small systems.

---

## What this is not

To be direct about scope, so expectations are accurate from page one:

- This is **not** a claim that First Nations partnership already exists. Section 06 (First Nations Vision) is presented honestly as an invitation and a commitment to proper, consent-based process — not as a completed consultation.
- This is **not** a claim that Susono programming is already running. Section 05 is a genuine proposal built on a real relationship, not a description of an existing exchange.
- This is **not** a request for council to take on technical risk. Every pilot project in Section 08 is scoped to be small, time-boxed, and built on technology B$S has already shipped and tested.
- This is **not** an attack on existing council art or placemaking investment. It is explicitly framed as building on it.
