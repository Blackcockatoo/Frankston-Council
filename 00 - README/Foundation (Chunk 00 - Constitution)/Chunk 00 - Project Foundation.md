# CHUNK 00 — PROJECT FOUNDATION
## The Constitution for "Frankston 2035 — Art That Lives"
### Blue Snake Studio

---

This document is the single source of truth for every other document, deck, poster, speech, and graphic produced under the *Frankston 2035 — Art That Lives* proposal. It exists so that twenty different writers — human or AI — working on twenty different chunks in parallel produce work that reads as if one creative director sat over every page.

**Rule zero:** if any future chunk contradicts this document, this document wins. If this document needs to change, the change happens here first, and every existing chunk is checked against it — not the other way around.

---

## 1. Project Purpose Statement

*Frankston 2035 — Art That Lives* exists to turn Frankston's public art and creative culture from something people walk past into something people walk *into* — a layer of civic life that teaches, travels between generations and cultures, and gives every child in Frankston a real chance to become a creator, not just a viewer.

The proposal asks Frankston City Council to back a staged, low-risk program — built on technology Blue Snake Studio has already designed and tested — that adds a living, interactive layer onto the city's existing investment in public art, education, and cultural connection.

---

## 2. Core Philosophy

**Art that stops at the wall is finished. Art that lives keeps going.**

Three beliefs underpin every page in this package:

1. **Creative infrastructure, not creative decoration.** A mural, a trail, or a story is infrastructure the same way a footpath or a library is — something the whole community can build on, return to, and add to over time. It should be designed and maintained with that level of seriousness.
2. **Respect before reach.** Cultural content — especially First Nations content and the Frankston–Susono relationship — only travels as far as its custodians are comfortable with, and never further. Scale is never the goal at the expense of consent.
3. **Childhood is not a market.** Every tool built for children prioritises attention, privacy, and offline-first design over engagement metrics. A healthy relationship with technology is a design requirement, not an afterthought.

---

## 3. Vision Statement

**By 2035, Frankston is the city where public art teaches.** Where a foreshore mural can be scanned by a Year 4 class and become a maths lesson. Where a story can be told in English and Japanese on the same trail. Where Bunurong/Boonwurrung language and knowledge are present in public space because the community chose to put them there, on their own terms. Where a child is as likely to make a piece of public creative work as to look at one.

This is a ten-year horizon reached through small, provable steps — never a single transformation announced and imposed.

---

## 4. Design Principles

These apply to every document, not just visual ones:

1. **One page, one message.** If a page needs two headlines, it needs to be two pages.
2. **Show the system, then the detail.** Every section opens with the big shape (a diagram, a single sentence, a timeline) before it opens with paragraphs.
3. **Earned trust over big claims.** Every claim in this package should be checkable. Where something is aspirational rather than proven, the document says so plainly — that honesty is a feature of the brand, not a weakness to hide.
4. **White space is a design element, not empty space.** Pages should never feel like they're trying to fit everything in.
5. **Generous to the reader's time.** Every long document has a short sibling (this is why Section 9 — Document Architecture — requires layered lengths for almost everything).

---

## 5. Tone & Voice Guide

**Tone:** Visionary, practical, civic, beautiful, community-first. Optimistic without being naive. Confident without being boastful.

**Voice rules:**

- Write the way a respected local architect or museum curator would speak to council — warm, precise, never corporate.
- Active voice. Concrete nouns. Avoid consultancy filler ("synergy," "leverage," "ecosystem of stakeholders," "unlock value").
- Never attack or imply criticism of Council or past decisions. Every reference to the status quo is framed as *foundation*, never *failure*.
- Never overclaim. Words like "partnership," "in collaboration with," or "co-designed with" are reserved exclusively for relationships that are real and consented to at time of publishing. Everything else uses "invitation," "proposal," "in the spirit of," or "we hope to."
- First Nations content is always written with humility, in the passive-invitation register, never the announcement register. Compare:
  - ❌ "Blue Snake Studio's First Nations program will bring Bunurong language to the foreshore."
  - ✅ "We would welcome the opportunity to be guided by Bunurong/Boonwurrung Traditional Owners on how, whether, and when language and story might be shared in public space."
- Numbers and statistics are only used when they are real and sourced. No invented percentages, no "studies show" without a study.

## 6. Document Architecture

Every chunk in this project is expected to be able to produce its content at up to seven depths, as requested by the Parallel Claude Prompt template. Not every chunk needs every depth — but where requested, depths are:

| Depth | Length | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Purpose Statement | 1–3 sentences | What this chunk is *for* |
| Main Document Draft | Full length | The complete, authoritative version |
| 1-Page Summary | ~300–400 words | Stand-alone, skimmable |
| Bullet-Point Version | Fragmented | For slides, quick briefings |
| Council-Safe Version | Politically reviewed | Removes anything that could read as overreach, criticism, or premature claim |
| Memetic / High-Impact Version | 1–3 lines | A single shareable idea — for social, posters, pull quotes |
| (Visual / Asset layer) | N/A | See Sections 13–14 below |

**Rule:** the Main Document Draft is always written first and is the single source other depths are compressed *from* — never written independently, to avoid drift between versions.

## 7. Naming Conventions

- **Project name:** *Frankston 2035 — Art That Lives* (capital F, capital A, capital T, capital L; em dash, not hyphen, between title and subtitle).
- **Studio name in running text:** "Blue Snake Studio" on first use per document; "the studio" or "B$S" thereafter is acceptable in internal/working documents only — public-facing documents always spell out "Blue Snake Studio."
- **File naming pattern:** `[Section Number] - [Section Name] - [Document Title] - [Depth].[ext]`
  Example: `08 - Pilot Projects - QR Trail - Council-Safe Version.pdf`
- **No version numbers in filenames** (see Section 15, Version Control) — the file system, not the filename, tracks versions.
- Section numbers always match the master Contents index (00–17) established in the README.

## 8. Branding Rules

- Blue Snake Studio's existing visual identity (sacred-geometry motifs, the "faster than lightning · slower than moss" tagline) is **not** the primary visual language of this proposal. This proposal is **civic-first** — Frankston's identity leads, B$S branding is a quiet, secondary credit (e.g. small footer credit: "A proposal by Blue Snake Studio"), never a co-brand competing with Council's own visual identity.
- No B$S sacred-geometry iconography appears on resident- or child-facing material unless it has been simplified into the shared icon system defined in Section 13.
- The Frankston Council crest/logo (if and when supplied by Council) always takes visual seniority over the B$S mark on any co-branded page.
- First Nations and Susono sections never carry the B$S logo above or beside any Traditional Owner or Susono civic mark — B$S branding sits below, smaller, credited last.

## 9. Information Hierarchy

Within any document, the hierarchy is always:

1. **The single message** (headline / thesis sentence)
2. **Why it matters to Frankston specifically** (not generic civic-innovation language)
3. **What already exists that this builds on** (acknowledgement before proposal)
4. **What is being proposed**
5. **How risk is contained** (staging, consent, cost)
6. **What happens next** (a specific, small next action — never "let's discuss")

No document skips step 3. This is what keeps the whole package from reading as critical of Council.

## 10. Success Metrics

Metrics are only ever stated where they are genuinely measurable and where the proposal commits to measuring them honestly — including if a pilot underperforms.

**Program-level (long-term, Stage 3–4):**
- Number of public art locations with a living/interactive layer
- Number of schools using Teacher Kits or Library Packs
- Evidence of genuinely community-authored (not just B$S-authored) content via the Community Creator Program
- Whether First Nations partnership exists at all, and on what terms set by Traditional Owners — presence of partnership, not volume of content, is the metric
- Whether the Susono relationship has produced at least one genuine bilingual exchange artifact

**Pilot-level (Stage 1, near-term):**
- QR scan counts and time-on-page (anonymous, aggregate only)
- Teacher and librarian qualitative feedback
- Cost-per-pilot versus budget

**What is deliberately NOT a metric:** engagement time for children's tools, daily active users, or any metric that would reward maximising a child's attention. This is a hard constraint, not a trade-off (see Section 07 — Healthy Technology Charter).

## 11. Audience Profiles

| Audience | What they need to hear first | What they're wary of |
|---|---|---|
| **Councillors** | Risk is staged and small; this builds on what Frankston already did right | Scope creep, ongoing cost, political risk from overclaiming First Nations partnership |
| **Council staff** | Clear, low-effort integration into existing programs; who does what | Being handed unscoped extra work |
| **Teachers** | Curriculum fit, low prep time, child safety/privacy by design | Another ed-tech tool that overpromises and under-delivers, or steals classroom time |
| **Students** | It's actually fun and they get to make something, not just look at something | Being talked down to |
| **Community members** | This is for them and their kids, and they can contribute, not just consume | Being a backdrop for a studio's tech showcase |
| **Sponsors / grant assessors** | Evidence-aware framing, real measurable outcomes, staged budget | Vague "impact" language with no measurement plan |

## 13. Colour & Typography Direction

**Palette — coastal Frankston, not generic tech-blue.** Drawn from Port Phillip Bay foreshore tones: pale bay-water, wet sand, dusk navy. Deliberately avoids the bright SaaS-gradient blue that every civic-tech deck defaults to.

| Token | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| `bay-deep` | `#1B3A4B` | Headlines, primary text, dark backgrounds |
| `bay-mid` | `#3E7186` | Secondary headings, icons, links |
| `bay-pale` | `#BFD7DD` | Backgrounds, panels, charts (light fill) |
| `sand` | `#E8E1D3` | Warm neutral background, alternates with white |
| `dusk-coral` | `#D98E73` | Single accent — pull-quotes, key stats, CTA highlights only |
| `paper` | `#FAFAF7` | Primary page background |
| `ink` | `#1A1A1A` | Body text on light backgrounds |

Rule: **dusk-coral is used once per page, maximum twice.** It marks the single most important thing on the page — never decoration.

**Typography:**
- **Display face:** a humanist serif with character (e.g. DM Serif Display, already used in B$S's Kṛṣṇapakṣi booklet work) — for titles and section openers only, used at large size, never for body text.
- **Body face:** a clean, highly legible sans (e.g. Inter or similar) for all running text, tables, and captions.
- **Accent/data face:** a monospace or semi-condensed sans for statistics, QR labels, and timeline markers — signals "this is a measured fact" versus prose.
- Generous line-height (1.5–1.6 for body). Headlines large and confident — this is a keynote, not a memo.

## 14. Asset Standards

- **Icons:** one consistent icon family across the whole package — simple line icons, single weight, rounded terminals (not B$S's sacred-geometry motifs in resident-facing contexts — see Section 8).
- **Photography:** full-bleed only where it serves the single-message rule (Section 4); always real Frankston imagery where possible, never generic stock-people-in-a-meeting photography.
- **Diagrams:** every roadmap, timeline, or process diagram uses the same visual grammar — numbered stages in `bay-mid` circles, connecting lines in `bay-pale`, single `dusk-coral` highlight only on the "you are here" stage.
- **QR codes:** always paired with a one-line plain-English label of what scanning leads to (never a bare code).
- **File formats for production:** working files in editable source (Markdown/Figma/native), exports to PDF (print) and PNG/SVG (digital), per the Print Ready (12) and Graphics (10) sections.

---

## 15. Version Control

- The **Contents** document (Section 00, README) is the only place that records what stage each section is at. Individual files are never named "v2," "final," "FINAL_v3."
- Every substantive change to a foundational document (this one, the Project Vision, or the Roadmap) is logged in a short changelog at the bottom of that document — date, what changed, why.
- Nothing is "final" until council has formally adopted it. Until then, every document is a draft, and should not visually present itself as a finished, official Council publication (no Council letterhead, no implied endorsement).

## 17. Future Expansion Rules

For any future chunk, section, or contributor (human or AI):

1. **Read this document and Section 00's Project Vision before writing anything.** If a new chunk's tone, claims, or structure conflict with either, the new chunk is wrong, not this document.
2. **Never introduce a new named program, partnership, or claim** without first checking Section 5 (Tone & Voice) and confirming it's real or properly hedged as aspirational.
3. **New sections beyond 00–17 are not added casually.** If genuinely needed, they're proposed as an addition to the Contents index first, with a one-line justification, before content is built.
4. **Every new visual asset uses the Section 13 palette and Section 14 standards** — no one-off colour or icon styles, even for a single poster.
5. **When in doubt about scope, under-claim.** It is always easier to expand a modest, honest proposal later than to retract an overclaimed one.
