# Foundation — 1-Page Summary
## *Frankston 2035 — Art That Lives* · Blue Snake Studio

**The idea:** Frankston turns its public art and creative culture from something people walk past into something people walk into — art that teaches, travels across generations and cultures, and helps every child become a creator.

**The philosophy:** Creative infrastructure, not decoration. Respect before reach. Childhood is not a market.

**The voice:** Visionary, practical, civic, beautiful, community-first. Active voice, no consultancy filler, no overclaiming. Council is never criticised — every past investment is framed as foundation, not failure.

**The hardest rule:** First Nations content is always written as an *invitation*, never an *announcement*. "We would welcome guidance from Traditional Owners," never "our First Nations program will…" The same caution applies to the Frankston–Susono relationship — propose, don't presume.

**The palette:** Coastal, not corporate. Bay-deep navy, bay-mid teal, pale bay-water, warm sand, and a single coral accent used once or twice per page — never decoration, always the one thing that matters most on that page.

**The type:** A characterful serif for titles only; a clean sans for everything people actually read; a monospace/data face wherever a number is doing real work.

**The structure every document follows:**
1. The single message
2. Why it matters to Frankston specifically
3. What already exists that this builds on
4. What's being proposed
5. How risk is contained
6. What happens next (small and specific)

**The naming pattern:** `[Section] - [Section Name] - [Title] - [Depth].[ext]` — no "v2" or "FINAL" in filenames. The Contents index, not the filename, tracks status.

**The non-negotiables:**
- No invented statistics or fake studies.
- No claimed partnerships that haven't given consent.
- No engagement-time metrics for children's tools — ever.
- Council branding always senior to B$S branding; Traditional Owner and Susono marks always senior to both.

**If in doubt:** under-claim. Re-read the full Chunk 00 document and the Project Vision before writing anything new.
