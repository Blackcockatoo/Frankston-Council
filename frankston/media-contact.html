Short press summary

Frankston 2035 - Art That Lives

Frankston 2035 is a civic creativity proposal that turns public art, schools, libraries, local memory, and healthy technology into small practical pathways for community participation.

The core idea is simple: Frankston is the artwork. Public art should not stop at being looked at. It should teach, invite, connect, and help new creators emerge.

The first recommended move is a low-risk print-first pilot, such as a Library Creative Pack, before any wider QR, app, or public-art rollout.
